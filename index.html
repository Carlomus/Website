Welcome to My Website

Hi, I’m Carlo, a Machine Learning Research Scientist currently working at Fractile, where I’m pushing the boundaries of LLM optimization on next-gen chips. Our goal is ambitious—a 100x boost in both speed and energy efficiency for large-scale model deployment. As one of Fractile’s earliest hires, I’ve worn many hats, from designing low-level circuits to leading compiler optimizations and shaping the high-level architecture of our software stack.

In a previous life, before ML, I was a physicist. I earned my Ph.D. in Particle and Accelerator Physics from the University of Oxford, working mainly at CERN, where I designed a low-energy beamline for the NA61/SHINE experiment. This project required a blend of simulation, data analysis, and collaboration, skills I now apply to my work in AI. Alongside research, I also enjoy mentoring.

My interests bridge generative AI, cross-modal AI models, quantization and exotic number formats for efficient model deployment. But let's be honest, as long as there are hard problems to solve, anything can be interesting!

On this site, you’ll find insights into my projects, research, and career path. Dive in to explore!

When I'm not working I'm usually playing chess (aiming for 2000, only 100 points to go!), bouldering, or cooking. In the winter I enjoy skiing, and in the summer I love scuba diving!.

Research

Working at an incredibly fast moving start up, it is hard to find a moment to breathe, let alone publish articles, but here are some of the projects I am currently working on:

  • Optimization of Large Language Models (LLMs): Developing methods to enhance the performance of LLMs, including quantization, sparsity, exotic number formats, and generative AI optimizations tailored to next-gen hardware.
  • Efficient Model Deployment: Designing scalable and efficient deployment frameworks for AI models, focusing on reducing energy consumption and computational overhead, while maintaining performance in diverse applications.
  • Predictive Maintenance with AI: Leveraging GNNs and other AI models to anticipate and prevent hardware failures in particle accelerators, contributing to improved uptime and reliability in high-energy physics experiments.
