Welcome to My Website!

Hi, I’m Carlo, a Machine Learning Research Scientist currently working at Fractile, where I’m pushing the boundaries of LLM optimization on next-gen chips. Our goal is ambitious—a 100x boost in both speed and energy efficiency for large-scale model deployment. As one of Fractile’s earliest hires, I’ve worn many hats, from designing low-level circuits to leading compiler optimizations and shaping the high-level architecture of our software stack.

In a previous life, before ML, I was a physicist. I earned my Ph.D. in Particle and Accelerator Physics from the University of Oxford, working mainly at CERN, where I designed a low-energy beamline for the NA61/SHINE experiment. This project required a blend of simulation, data analysis, and collaboration, skills I now apply to my work in AI.

My interests bridge generative AI, cross-modal AI models, quantization and exotic number formats for efficient model deployment. But let's be honest, as long as there are hard problems to solve, everything is interesting!

Research

Working at an incredibly fast-moving startup, it is hard to find a moment to breathe, let alone publish articles, but here are some of the projects I am currently working on:

  • Optimization of Large Language Models (LLMs): Developing methods to enhance LLM performance, including quantization, sparsity, and exotic number formats tailored to next-gen hardware.
  • Efficient Model Deployment: Designing scalable frameworks for AI model deployment, reducing energy consumption while maintaining high performance.
  • Predictive Maintenance with AI: Leveraging GNNs and AI models to anticipate and prevent hardware failures in particle accelerators, improving uptime and reliability.
